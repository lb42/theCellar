Draft Charge for the Workgroup on Character Encoding TEI ED W72
14 April 2002

Objectives

  1. Reformulate this list of objectives.
  2. Review thoroughly all aspects of the recommendations currently presented in TEI P4 with respect to the encoding of character-level information and of writing systems in general;
  3. Make specific proposals for changes in the existing recommendations which will better align the TEI Recommendations with current needs and practices in the widest text enoding community;
  4. Draft new recommendations in areas not at present addressed explicitly or adequately by the Guidelines; such areas include (but are not limited to)
    • mechanisms for the definition of character semantics in a Unicode context;
    • assessment of input and display software for multilingual work;
    • best practice in the standardized encoding of manuscript materials;
  5. Respond to comments on the relevant portions of TEI P4 routed to this work group by the editors.

Administrative

The initial membership of this workgroup included the following:

  • Christian Wittern, Kyoto University (wittern@kanji.zinbun.kyoto-u.ac.jp) : chair
  • Deborah Anderson, Berkeley (dwanders@socrates.berkeley.edu)
  • David Birnbaum, Pittsburgh University (djbpitt+@pitt.edu)
  • Patrick Durusau, Emory University (pdurusau@emory.edu)

The chair should specify current membership as soon as possible, for ratification by the TEI Board at its next meeting. The TEI Editors are ex officio members of all TEI Work Groups. Other constraints and procedures applicable to all TEI Work Groups are described in document TEI ED W54.

The group has a budget of USD 8000 for the year ending 2003, during which one meeting is authorized.

The Work group is required to provide progress reports on its work regularly to the TEI Technical Council, which has authority for approving all technical output from the work group.

Deadlines

(To be agreed with WG head):

  • Reformulation of objectives and recruitment of work group members should be completed by 1 May 2002, in time for the TEI Board meeting May 9th;
  • A preliminary work plan should be prepared by 1 August 2002
  • A first draft of recommendations for revisions and additions required to P4 should be prepared by 15 September 2002, for review by the TEI Council meeting of 11 Oct 2002.
  • The final reports from the Workgroup should be completed by 1 January 2003
