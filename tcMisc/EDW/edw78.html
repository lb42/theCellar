Proposals for Amendments

Introduction

The Text Encoding Initiative Consortium (TEI-C) already has mechanisms in place

  • to effect major strategic technical changes to the Guidelines, (e.g. adding new chapters or overhauling existing ones, deciding on what metalanguage(s) will be used, specific major issues like character encoding etc). Such changes are the responsibility of the TEI Council, mostly by commissioning work groups
  • to correct corrigible errors in the Guidelines; such changes are generally effected by the Editors on their own.

But there does not exist any mechanism for considering and effecting changes that are in between: i.e. not arising from something demonstrably erroneous, but too minor to require a work group be commisioned. Examples might be to eliminate the <dateRange>> and <timeRange> elements; to change the verse and drama tagsets to be additional instead of base; to provide a few extra elements for encoding archival correspondence.

This document suggests a mechanism both for users of the TEI scheme to recommend such changes, and for the TEI-C to consider and implement them.

Proposals for Amendments

Users can submit a proposal for an amendment at will via the web. The interface is yet to be determined, but it will probably involve a web-form or submission of an XML file, conforming to a specification yet to be determined by the editors.

Proposals for Amendment do not have to be very technical or very detailed, but should be clear and coherent. They should contain, at a minimum, the following information:

  • A clear description of the problems or shortcomings which need to be addressed
  • A clear summary of any proposed modification to the Guidelines which would address the stated problem
  • name and contact information of the proposer
  • name and contact information of someone willing to work on implementing the proposal (may be the same person as the proposer)
  • An estimate of the likely consequences of implementing the proposal
  • An estimate of the likely amount of work involved in implementing the proposal

The editors will check all submitted proposals, and will jointly determine whether or not the proposal is complete and worthy of further consideration. This is the `grand jury' stage, which is intended to weed out complete crackpot or spam requests (e.g., that the TEI should devote a base tag set to the encoding of the recorded mating calls of extinct species). It doesn't take much to pass muster here, but those that don't are not even submitted to publicly available scrutiny.

On acceptance, the editors will register the Proposal for Change in a TEI standing document, using a format to be determined.

It is the job of the Council to determine dispositionl of Proposals for Change. At least twice a year, the Council will consider the merits of all currently submitted proposals in a gathered field, and determine their disposition. Likely outcomes for a proposal are one of the following:

  1. it is inappropriate to proceed further with the proposal because it is out of scope for the TEI Guidelines, technically flawed, or prohibitively complex or expensive
  2. the proposal needs further technical work before it can be integrated into or used to modify the current Guidelines
  3. The proposal identifies a modification or other course of action which can be integrated directly into the current Guidelines

The Council may identify other kinds of disposition in the light of experience, but these seem the most probable.

The editors will record the Council's decision on each proposal in its associated documentation, and these will be available as a matter of public record from the TEI web site. Taking each type of disposition in turn,

  • In the case of a rejected proposal, the Council will supply the editors with explanation of their grounds for the decision for inclusion in the documentation. No further action is taken.
  • In the case of a proposal needing further work, the Council will assign at least one of its members to work with the person indicated in the proposal and with the editors to carry out such further work. Once the work is completed, the proposal is resubmitted to the Council, in the same way as any other proposal.
  • In the case of a proposal not needing further work, the editors will proceed directly to implementation, without further reference to the Council.
  • When a proposal for change is implemented, its associated record will be integrated into the change list for the relevant edition of the Guidelines.
